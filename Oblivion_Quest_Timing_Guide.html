Oblivion Quest Timing Guide

This interactive guide helps you decide when to tackle quests in The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion for optimal rewards and challenge.

Main Quest

  • Start early (Level 1–4) to access Kvatch and Sigil Stones.
  • Finish later (Level 20+) for stronger Sigil Stone enchantments.

Fighter’s Guild

  • Recommended Level: 5–15 for balanced enemies and loot.

Thieves Guild

  • Recommended Level: 10–20 for better loot and fence access.

Mages Guild / Arcane University

  • Start early (Level 1–5) to unlock spell crafting and enchantments.

Dark Brotherhood

  • Do before Level 20 for full gear value.

Arena

  • Start early (Level 1–5); enemies scale.

Knights of the Nine (DLC)

  • Start around Level 10–20; relics max out at Level 21.

Shivering Isles (DLC)

  • Ideal at Level 15–25 for balance of challenge and loot.

Daedric Quests (selected)

  • Azura (2+): Azura's Star is always useful.
  • Mehrunes Dagon (20+): Ruin’s Edge best at 20.
  • Boethia (20+): Requires Level 20.
  • Clavicus Vile (20+): Strongest Masque version.
  • Umbra (15–20): Tough enemy, strong weapon.

Unique/Notable Quests

  • "Whodunit?" – Any level.
  • "Umbra" – Best at 15+ for manageable challenge.
  • "Nocturnal" – ASAP at Level 10+ for Skeleton Key.

Max-Level Loot Planning

  • Skeleton Key – Level 10 (fixed)
  • Azura’s Star – No scaling
  • Umbra Sword – No scaling
  • Spellbreaker – Level 20
  • Masque of Clavicus Vile – Level 20
  • Wabbajack – Level 20
  • Savior’s Hide – Level 20
  • Crusader's Relics – Level 21
